About Me

My name is Sam Riddle, and I am a computer science student at the University of British Columbia. I am a well-versed software developer with many years of experience, but am always looking for new projects and challenges to tackle.

I have been programming with Java since I was fifteen years old, and have made several projects, large and small. I also have lots of experience with C, GoLang and Python, and have worked with C++, C# and JavaScript as well. I have also been working as a Teaching Assistant for computer science classes for over a year.

I am very interested in programming and designing for lower level technologies and hardware, such as operating systems, kernels, CPU and GPU design, and optimization. I also love working with higher level languages, and seeing how they work under the hood.

My other interests include working with physical hardware, mostly personal computers; playing video games, with some degree of competitiveness; and I follow the stock market and make investments, although I am not particularly good at it yet.
